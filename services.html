FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services and processes.

How do I get started with buying a home?
Getting started is easy! Simply contact us through our website or call our office. We'll schedule a free consultation where we'll discuss your goals, budget, timeline, and preferences. From there, we'll assign you a dedicated agent who specializes in your target area and price range.
What areas do you serve?
Aura Realty currently operates in 12 major metropolitan areas across the United States, including Miami, New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Boston, Austin, San Francisco, Seattle, and more. We're continuously expanding to new markets to better serve our clients.
Do you offer virtual property tours?
Absolutely! We offer both 3D virtual tours and live video walkthroughs for all of our listed properties. This is especially helpful for out-of-state buyers or those with busy schedules. Contact your agent to schedule a virtual viewing at your convenience.
What fees do buyers typically pay?
In most transactions, the seller pays the real estate commission, meaning buyers can use our services at no additional cost. However, buyers should budget for closing costs (typically 2-5% of the purchase price), which include appraisal fees, title insurance, and other processing fees. Your agent will provide a detailed breakdown early in the process.
How long does the home buying process take?
The timeline varies depending on market conditions and individual circumstances. On average, the process from initial search to closing takes 60-90 days. However, with pre-approval in hand and a motivated seller, some transactions can close in as little as 30 days. Your agent will keep you informed every step of the way.
Can you help with financing and mortgages?
While we are not a lending institution, we have established partnerships with top mortgage lenders and can connect you with trusted loan officers who offer competitive rates. We also provide guidance on pre-approval, down payment assistance programs, and financial planning resources.
